  Description:
    Abstract:
      This file consists of ancestry data (first ancestry reported) for Indiana county subdivisions (legal townships, 
	  also known as "Minor Civil Divisions") derived from the U.S. Census Bureau's Summary File 3 (SF3) and total 
	  population, race, and ethnicity data (single race) from the U.S. Census Bureau's Summary File 1 (SF1). 

      The ancestry data are from SF3, table PCT16, First Ancestry Reported. These data are based on questions in the 
	  population and housing long census questionnaire, also known as the "Sample Data" because they are collected 
	  from a sample of the population (generally 1-in-6). Ancestry is a person's self-identified heritage, ethnic 
	  origin, descent, or close identification to an ethnic group. Examples of ancestry groups include Arab, Brazilian, 
	  Canadian, Czech, Irish, Italian, Russian, Subsaharan African, West Indian, etc.

      The Race and Ethnicity data are derived from SF 1, tables P1, P3 and P4, which is based on questions asked on the 
	  short Census questionnaire which is sent to the entire population. Race is a person’s self-identified race or 
	  races with which respondent most closely identifies. The Census Bureau recognizes 5 race classes: White, Black 
	  or African-American, American Indian or Alaska Native, Asian and Native Hawaiian or other Pacific Islander. The 
	  Race data used consists of data for people of one race alone. 

      Ethnicity (origin) is the heritage, nationality group, lineage, or country of birth of the person or the person's 
	  parents or ancestors before their arrival in the United States. People who identified their origin as Spanish, 
	  Hispanic, or Latino in their answers to the short census questionnaire are reported as Hispanic.

      These data were downloaded from the U.S. Census Bureau’s American Factfinder search engine.
	  
    Purpose:
      CENSUS_MCD_ANCESTRY_ETHNICITY_IN.SHP was developed to allow race, ethnicity, and ancestry for
	  Indiana county subdivisions (minor civil divisions) to be mapped using ArcGIS.

    Process_Step:
      Process_Description:
        The file CS18_D00_E00.ZIP was downloaded from the U.S. Census Bureau and unzipped. ESRI ArcToolBox was then used to convert the ESRI interchange
        file CS18_D00.E00 to an ESRI coverage named "MCD_IN." MCD_IN was then converted to a shapefile named MCD_IN.SHP.
        ESRI ArcToolBox "Define Projection Wizard" was then used on MCD_IN.SHP to define the projection provided, which was Geographic
        (Lat/Long) NAD83. ESRI ArcToolBox "Project Wizard" was then used to reproject to UTM Zone 16, NAD83, and the output shapefile
        was named CENSUS_MCD_ANCESTRY_ETHNICITY_IN.SHP. ESRI ArcMap was then used to edit the shapefile, and the following fields were
        dropped from the attribute table: "Area," "Perimeter," "MCD_IN_," "MCD_IN_I," "State," "LSAD," and "LSAD_TRANS."

        Two additional edits were made to the attribute table using ESRI ArcMap. Names of Indiana counties were added to a field
        named "Cnty_Name," using the "Join" function, and the field named "Name" was re-named as "MCD_Name."
